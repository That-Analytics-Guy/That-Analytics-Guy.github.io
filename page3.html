Credit Default Risk Prediction for Non-Banking Financial Companies

Non-banking Financial Companies like Home Credit are the financial institutions that does not have a full banking license or is not supervised by a national or international banking regulatory agency. As a result, these institutions do not have access to the credit bureaus to get the applicant’s credit score. They must rely on their own credit management database and determine the financial capability for each of their loan applicant. A machine learning algorithm to analyse the credit risk can lessen the severity of loss to such institutions. Although there are several research and machine learning algorithms available to narrow down the possible loan defaulters, the aim of this project is to improve the accuracy of credit risk analysis by using the best suitable machine learning technique and considering a broad scale of factors/variables associated with the applicant. This will facilitate the Non-banking Financial Institutions with the applicant’s detailed insight for making an informed decision. 

1

Goal

The overall aim of the project is to build a PD model (Probability of Default) for the NBFC’s which helps them to make an informed decision on the Loan defaulters

2

Approach

Using the CRISP DM methodology, after understanding the business and data. Data Preprocessing was done in order to clean and remove outliers. Further the modeling part consisted of 2 steps
a. Feauture Selection - The first part, aims in identifying the major variables that should be taken care for our final prediction as more number of variables would result in reducing the accuracy of our model.

b. Developing models - The Second part, tries to build various machine learning models after identifying the various predictor variables from the first step.

3

Result

Selection of the best model by comparing the accuracies and error rate of various models generated.
